Places to Visit In & Around Karaikal

Karaikal is a land of spirituality, history and natural beauty. From ancient temples to peaceful beaches, there is something for every traveller.

In Karaikal Town

🛕
Temple · 1.5 km from station

Karaikal Ammaiyar Temple

Dedicated to the celebrated woman Saiva saint Punithavathi, one of the 63 Nayanmars. Famous for the Mangani festival celebrated every year in June–July.

🛕
Temple · City centre

Sri Kailasanathar Temple

Around 2000 years old, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Reconstructed by Pallava rulers in the 8th century and again during French rule. Famous for its annual Karaikal Chariot Festival.

🛕
Temple · 1.5 km from station

Sri Nithyakalyana Perumal Temple

Dedicated to Lord Vishnu with a unique reclining idol carved from a single stone. Festive days include Rama Navami, Vaikunta Ekadasi and Hanuman Jayanti.

⛪
Church · City centre

Our Lady of Angels Church

Built in the 18th century during French rule. Known for its stained glass windows, marble altar, and a 133-feet tower with three huge clocks and five large bells — the tallest landmark of Karaikal.

🌊
Beach · 1.5 km from station

Karaikal Beach & Eco Mangrove Forest

One of the finest beaches in South Tamil Nadu along the Arasalar River. Features water sports (boating, kayaking), a children's park, restaurants, tennis court and a vibrant mangrove forest with migratory birds.

⛵
Leisure · 2 km from town

Arasalar River Boat House

Situated on the backwaters of the Arasalar River. Offers peddler boats, motor boats and rowing boats. Beach Road connecting the town to the sea is beautifully illuminated at night.

🕌
Dargah · 1.2 km from station

Mastan Saheb Syed Dawood Dargah

A sacred Muslim shrine housing the tomb of Syed Dawood, a saint from Bukhara. Celebrated with the colourful annual Kandoori festival featuring processions, music and fireworks.

Around Karaikal

🛕
Temple · 6 km west

Thirunallar — Sri Saneeswarar Temple

One of the most famous Shani (Saturn) temples in all of India. Houses a rare Emerald Lingam. The Sainpezarchi festival attracts over 3 lakh devotees.

🛕
Temple · 8 km

Aayiram Kali Amman Temple

Ancient temple dedicated to Goddess Kali, famous for its sacred wooden idol said to have been found floating in the sea in a silver box.

🛕
Temple · 15 km

Ambagarathur — Bhadrakali Temple

Dedicated to Goddess Parasakthi. Features an impressive Rajagopura, wide corridors and a large mandapa.

🛕
Temple · 15 km north

Thirumeeyachur — Mear Temple

One of the 275 Paadal Petra Sthalams. Dedicated to Lord Shiva and Lalithambika.

🛕
Temple · 6 km · Melakasakudy

Varadaraja Perumal Temple

Built in the 12th century and protected as an ancient monument. Renowned for beautiful stone carvings.

⛪
Pilgrimage · 20 km

Velankanni — Basilica of Our Lady

Known as the "Lourdes of the East", this famous Catholic basilica draws millions of pilgrims every year.

🌊
Heritage town · 14 km

Tharangambadi (Tranquebar)

A charming Danish colonial town with a historic fort, old churches and a stunning beach.

🛕
Temple town · 20 km

Nagapattinam Temples

Coastal city with several ancient temples including Sri Kayahorana Swami Temple and the scenic beaches.
